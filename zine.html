D2 · Digital Zine · live

Fourteen pages,
three acts,
one driver.

A long-read for marketing professionals — the same audience the survey targeted. Designed to be opened on a phone in five minutes or on a desktop in fifteen. Every section closes with a "what this means for your campaign" line, not a glossary entry. Three short acts: what is this thing, how it works for one driver, what stops it from happening.

Artifact spec · D2

What the zine is, in numbers.

Built to a fixed shape so the production work fits the timeline and so the comprehension check has a defensible structure.

length

14pp

Pages, designed in three short acts. Each act fits in a single session.

structure

3/3

Acts — definition, walk-through, barriers. No glossary, no appendix.

read time

~12min

Median target for desktop reading. Phone read clocks in around five.

check

1Q

Embedded comprehension check on the closing page. Yes/No format.

Three acts · pp. 1–14

What's inside.

Each act answers a specific question a marketing professional should be asking before they propose a digital twin pilot to their leadership. The acts read in order, but each is self-contained.

ACT_01
pp. 1–4

What is this thing?

A working definition that is not the McKinsey definition. Plus three things a digital twin is not, so the reader stops confusing it with a CRM segment, a dashboard, or an A/B test.

Definition4 pages~3 min
ACT_02
pp. 5–10

How does it work for one driver?

A walk-through of one Ontario customer journey: data sources, the simulated outcomes, the personalized offer they see, and what specifically changed in pricing, coverage, and marketing message.

Walk-through6 pages~6 min
ACT_03
pp. 11–14

What stops it from happening?

The three barriers the survey surfaced — cost, ROI, privacy — addressed honestly, without dressing them up. Plus the comprehension check on the last page.

Barriers4 pages~3 min
The job of the zine is not to convince anyone that digital twins are the future. It's to make sure that the next time the phrase comes up in a meeting, one person in the room can describe what it actually does.

Closing page · embedded widget

What the comprehension check measures.

The last page of the zine carries one Yes/No question. The threshold is set against the D1 baseline — 73% of surveyed practitioners currently cannot explain digital twins. The check is anonymous and captured via a lightweight widget embedded on the closing page.

Comprehension check · zine closing page

Prompt: "I now understand at least one concrete use case for digital twins in auto insurance."

Options: Yes · No

Threshold: Yes ≥ 70% of respondents.

Baseline: D1 finding — 73% currently cannot explain the concept.

Status: instrument live alongside the zine.

Locked 2026-05-06

Success criteria for the zine.

Four directional thresholds. Not statistical claims — the lines below which the artifact is not working as intended. Tracked via scroll-depth analytics, GA4 page-time, file-download events, and the embedded comprehension widget.

The zine works if…

  1. Read-through: ≥60% of web-zine visitors scroll to ≥80% of zine length.
  2. Time on page: median ≥2 minutes (zine is read, not bounced).
  3. PDF downloads: ≥10 unique downloads in 14 days post-publication.
  4. Comprehension lift: ≥70% Yes on the closing question — "I now understand at least one concrete use case for digital twins in auto insurance."

Anchored against the D1 baseline (73% currently cannot explain digital twins). Cross-referenced in the methodology.
