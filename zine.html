The digital zine

A 14-page long-read, designed to be opened on a phone in five minutes or on a desktop in fifteen. It explains the auto insurance personalization use case for marketing professionals — the same audience the survey targeted. Every section ends with a "what this means for your campaign" line, not a glossary entry.

What's inside

14 pages, three movements

The zine is structured as three short acts. Each act answers a specific question a marketing professional should be asking before they propose a digital twin pilot to their leadership.

Act 1 · pp. 1–4

What is this thing?

A definition that is not the McKinsey definition. Plus three things a digital twin is not, so you stop confusing it with a CRM segment.

Act 2 · pp. 5–10

How does it work for one driver?

A walk through one Ontario customer journey: data sources, simulation, the offer they see, and what changed.

Act 3 · pp. 11–14

What stops it from happening?

Cost, ROI, privacy — the three barriers the survey surfaced — addressed honestly. Plus the comprehension check on the last page.

Success criteria for this artifact

The zine works if…

  1. Read-through: ≥60% of web-zine visitors scroll to ≥80% of zine length.
  2. Time on page: median ≥2 minutes (zine is read, not bounced).
  3. PDF downloads: ≥10 unique downloads in 14 days post-publication.
  4. Comprehension lift: ≥70% Yes on the closing question — "I now understand at least one concrete use case for digital twins in auto insurance."

Anchored against the D1 baseline (73% currently cannot explain digital twins). Implementation: scroll-depth analytics, embedded Yes/No widget at end of zine.
