The literature behind the project.

★ Peer-reviewed academic — 9

Peer-reviewed

The empirical and theoretical core. Where this project's claims about digital twins as personalization technology, customer experience tools, and underwriting instruments come from.

★ #1 · 2024 · Open access

Bäumer et al. — Mirroring Privacy Risks with Digital Twins

SN Computer Science, 5, Article 1109. Demonstrates how disparate open-data fragments combine into a complete personal profile via a digital twin, and proposes a pseudonymization-and-synthetic-data framework.

Themes: privacy · ethics · consent

Open at Springer ↗

★ #2 · 2025 · UNF Library

Hornik & Rachamim — AI-enabled Consumer Digital Twins

Management Review Quarterly, advance online publication. Conceptual platform for AI-built consumer digital twins using the MODA framework. Defines the consumer twin as a "synthetic agent" replicating cognition, preferences, and decision-making.

Themes: customer engagement · personalization · AI

Springer (paywalled — UNF Library access) ↗

★ #3 · 2025 · UNF Library

McColl-Kennedy et al. — Digital Twins: A Game Changer in CX

Journal of Service Management, advance online publication. Introduces digital twins to CX literature with a conceptual framework and roadmap; demonstrates applicability in B2B shipping, retail food, and healthcare.

Themes: customer experience · service management · personalization

Emerald (paywalled — UNF Library access) ↗

★ #4 · 2024 · Open access

Mitu & Zglavoci — Finance 4.0: Digital Twin Technology

The Young Economists Journal, 42, 23–32. Bibliometric analysis of 8,942 Web of Science articles on digital twins. Cites Altair: 50% of BFSI use DT for behavioral modeling.

Themes: finance · adoption · bibliometric

RePEc / Young Economists Journal ↗

★ #5 · 2024 · Open access

Oehlschläger et al. — How Digital Twins Impact Responsiveness

Schmalenbach Journal of Business Research, 76(5). Applies Information Processing Theory to show how digital twins help organizations respond faster to customer demand fluctuations through real-time virtual–physical linkage.

Themes: business value · responsiveness · sales/underwriting

Open at Springer ↗

★ #6 · 2026 · UNF Library

Quach et al. — Digital Twins: Enhancing Personalized Experience Through Phygital Journey

Australasian Marketing Journal, 34(1). Uses 27,148 Foursquare users and 2M+ check-ins across four cities to build digital twins via social network graphs. The most direct empirical source on personalization through DTs.

Themes: personalization · phygital · marketing journey

SAGE (paywalled — UNF Library access) ↗

★ #7 · 2024 · Open access (PDF)

Rehman — Digital Twins: A New Era in P&C Insurance Underwriting

International Journal of Science and Research, 13(6), 1072–1077. The single peer-reviewed paper directly on DT in insurance. Real-time monitoring of physical assets via continuously-updated virtual replicas.

Themes: insurance · underwriting · core source

Direct PDF (IJSR) ↗

★ #8 · 2024 · Open access

Soler-Dorda et al. — Privacy and Security Challenges of the Digital Twin: A Systematic Review

Journal of Universal Computer Science, 30(12), 1782–1806. Systematic review of 47 publications. Six categories of risk: data privacy, security, governance, infrastructure/standards, ethics, legal/social.

Themes: privacy · security · risk taxonomy

Open at J.UCS ↗

★ #9 · 2024 · Open access

Tao et al. — Digital Twin: A Review of the Evolution from Concept to Technology

Applied Sciences, 14(13), 5454. Comprehensive review of DT evolution from NASA's 2010 concept to current cross-industry technology. The reference for project introduction and background.

Themes: technology overview · history · cross-industry

Open at MDPI ↗

Industry reports — 5

Industry research

Quantitative adoption and market data. Where the survey-vs-industry-data comparison in the dashboard comes from.

#10 · 2023 · Registration required

Altair — 2023 Global Digital Twin Survey: BFSI

Survey of 2,000+ specialists. 71% of BFSI already use DTs; 97% rate them critical; 64% are "highly knowledgeable"; 50% use DTs for behavioral modelling. The source that specifically pushed this project to drop the "low awareness" framing.

Themes: adoption · BFSI · behavioral modeling · core source

Altair (registration) ↗

#11 · 2023 · Registration required

Capgemini & Qorus — World P&C Insurance Report 2023

The shift from insuring assets to insuring mobility. 42% of customers want a single multi-modal mobility policy; 63% of insurers worry about technology adequacy; only 21% have advanced partnership capabilities.

Themes: market context · personalization · P&C

Capgemini ↗

#12 · 2024 · Free with registration

KPMG — Advancing AI Across Insurance

Forecasts a $79B AI-in-insurance market by 2033. Identifies the gap between investment and adoption; interviews with Generali, PassportCard, Prudential, Zurich. Anchors the broader AI context for DTs.

Themes: AI · insurance transformation · adoption gap

KPMG ↗

#13 · 2024 · Summary free, full report paid

Market Research Future — Digital Twin Financial Services & Insurance Market 2024–2032

Market sizing: USD 3.45B (2023) → USD 10.5B (2032). Used quantitatively in the introduction and background of the project.

Themes: market sizing · forecast

MRFR ↗

#14 · 2023 · Open access

U.S. GAO — Science & Tech Spotlight: Digital Twins

Government technology overview written for non-specialists. Plain-language explanation of what DTs are, how they work, where they're used, what risks they introduce. Authoritative non-vendor source.

Themes: technology overview · risk · plain-language

GAO-23-106453 ↗

Regulatory — 2

Regulators on consent and privacy

The legal-and-policy frame. Specifically what regulators in the EU and Canada are saying about consent in data-rich personalization regimes.

#15 · 2024 · EU regulator

EDPB — Opinion 08/2024 on "Consent or Pay" Models

European Data Protection Board: "consent or pay" models on large platforms do not satisfy GDPR. Consent must be free, informed, specific. Binary "agree or pay" is not free consent. Direct read-across to DT-driven personalization.

Themes: GDPR · consent · privacy · ethics

EDPB Opinion ↗

#16 · 2023 · Canadian regulator

OPC Canada — 2022–23 Departmental Results Report

Annual report from Canada's Privacy Commissioner. Three pillars: privacy as fundamental right, privacy as innovation enabler, privacy as trust accelerator. Notes AI-company investigations and Bill C-27 work.

Themes: PIPEDA · Canada · Bill C-27 · regulatory context

OPC Canada ↗

Professional publications — 5

Practitioner perspective

Industry voices — insurer-published, consultant-published, and trade-press. Used for grounding in present-day practice and for triangulating the academic findings against vendor and operator perspectives.

#17 · 2024 · Insurer

MAPFRE — The Use of Digital Twins in Insurance

Practitioner-facing overview from a major international insurer. Covers DT use in auto (telematics + behavior-based personalization), health, and property insurance. Concrete examples from inside the industry.

Themes: insurer practice · auto · personalization

MAPFRE ↗

#18 · 2022 · Consultancy

McKinsey — Digital Twins: What Could They Do for Your Business?

Strategic primer on DTs from a major consulting firm. Definition, business value, implementation examples. Used for context but explicitly not used as a definition source — the project rejects "the McKinsey definition" frame in the zine.

Themes: business value · adoption · consulting

McKinsey ↗

#19 · 2024 · Tech vendor

NTT Data — Reimagining Insurance Risk Assessment with Digital Twins

Argues for a shift from static actuarial tables to real-time dynamic risk modelling via DTs. Direct application to underwriting — one of the audiences our project specifically excluded after the audience-narrowing iteration.

Themes: risk · underwriting · real-time modeling

NTT Data ↗

#20 · 2024 · Policy commentary

TechPolicy Press — Digital Twins Demand a New Social Contract

Argues that mass creation of digital twins of people requires a new social contract: new rules on consent, transparency, and data control. Introduces the Minimally Viable Permissibility Principle (MVPP).

Themes: ethics · social contract · consent design

TechPolicy Press ↗

#21 · 2024 · Trade press

The Actuary — The Power of Two: Digital Twins in Insurance

Profession-internal review for actuaries. How DTs can be used in risk modeling, pricing, and claims management. The view from inside the actuarial profession on what DT adoption changes.

Themes: actuarial · pricing · claims

The Actuary ↗

Practical note

How to access these sources

  • Open access (14 of 21): click the link, the PDF or full text is free. This includes all GAO, EDPB, OPC Canada, the open-access peer-reviewed papers, and the professional/trade publications.
  • Paywalled academic (3 of 21): Hornik & Rachamim (Springer), McColl-Kennedy et al. (Emerald), Quach et al. (SAGE). Access through a university library: at UNF, this is via library.unf.edu ↗ → Databases → SpringerLink / Emerald Insight / SAGE Journals.
  • Registration-gated (3 of 21): Altair, Capgemini, KPMG. Free, but require an email-form sign-up before download.
  • Commercial (1 of 21): MRFR market report — only the executive summary is free; the full report is paid.

Last verified 2026-04-30. Some publisher URLs may shift over time; if a link breaks, the DOI or title-and-author search will still resolve.
