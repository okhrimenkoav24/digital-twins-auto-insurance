Research question

What this project asks.

How can a multichannel digital media campaign be used to demonstrate the practical application and perceived value of digital twin technology to insurance marketing professionals in Ontario, Canada, based on industry data from 2022 to 2025? — Locked 2026-04-17, refined from professor-approved version

The question was narrowed across three iterations. The original framing spoke to "insurance professionals," then "marketing, sales, and underwriting," and finally "marketing professionals only." The final scope was approved by Dr. Wael Brahim on 2026-04-15.

Research design

Two layers of evidence, one use case.

The project pairs a secondary literature layer (Ontario industry 2022–2025) with a primary survey of practitioners. Together they ground every claim made across the six artifacts. The survey is the original empirical contribution; the industry layer keeps the practitioner findings from floating free of context.

Dimension Specification
Instrument 7 questions · single & multi-select · 5-pt scales · 1 open-ended
Sample N = 15 · Ontario · marketing role · convenience
Period 2026-03-25 → 2026-04-22
Analysis descriptive statistics + thematic coding of open-ended
Industry layer 21 sources · 9 peer-reviewed · 5 industry reports · 2 regulatory · 5 professional
Ethics REB-exempt class project · no PII collected · anonymised reporting
Sample · N=15

Who answered, and how they were reached.

The survey targeted insurance marketing professionals in Ontario, Canada. Recruitment ran from 2026-03-25 to 2026-04-22 across four channels, each with a distinct selection bias.

  • Warm introductions — 3 contacts, 6 responses. Skews toward people who already trust the researcher.
  • LinkedIn DMs to first-degree connections in the industry — ~20 sent, 4 responses.
  • Four Facebook groups — Insurance Talk Ontario Canada, Marketing for Insurance Agents, Leads Marketing and Automations, Insurance Ontario Canada. 40+ direct invites, 5 responses. Skews toward more publicly active practitioners.
  • Open call on the project's LinkedIn page — ~0 attributable responses.

The dashboard reports results without channel-stratification because at N=15 the strata are too small to be informative. The cost-of-implementation finding (60%) is the result that appears most stable across channels; the others may shift with a larger sample.

Why this scope

Eight artifacts became six.

Consolidating from eight artifacts to six was a research-judgment decision, not a scope cut. An infographic series and a short-form video would have re-stated material already covered by the zine and the LinkedIn campaign — adding production cost without adding evidentiary value. — Project log, 2026-04-08

The infographic content was absorbed into the zine (D2). The short-form video became the opening posts of the LinkedIn campaign (D6). The remaining six artifacts each handle one stage of a single pipeline: research → explain → demonstrate → interpret → assemble → distribute. Every artifact answers the same research question through a different medium, on the principle that a multichannel campaign is the right test of whether the message lands across formats — not whether the project produced the maximum number of things.

Closing the Assignment 1 gap

Success criteria — by artifact.

The lines below which each artifact would be accepted as not working as intended. Directional, not statistically powered.

D1 · Survey + Dashboard

  1. Capability shift: measurable reduction in the "cannot explain" tier between baseline and follow-up wave.
  2. Engagement: ≥40% of dashboard visitors scroll to the methodology section.
  3. Trust: ≥1 substantive citation or share by an industry peer, 2026-05 to 2026-06.
  4. Coverage: dashboard load <3s on cable; all charts render with no console errors.

D2 · Digital Zine

  1. Read-through: ≥60% reach 80% of zine length.
  2. Time on page: median ≥2:00.
  3. PDF downloads: ≥10 unique in 14 days.
  4. Comprehension lift: ≥70% Yes on embedded mini-survey closing question.

D3 · Decision Simulator

  1. Completion: ≥50% complete one full scenario.
  2. Time on artifact: median ≥2:00.
  3. Comprehension lift: ≥5 of 15 follow-ups can describe the mechanic in their own words.
  4. Stability: WebGL build runs on Chrome / Safari / Firefox latest, no crash in first 5 sessions.

D4 · Podcast Episode

  1. Listen-through: ≥40% completion rate.
  2. Reach: ≥50 unique plays in 14 days.
  3. Engagement: ≥3 substantive comments or DMs.
  4. Distribution: published on both Spotify and Apple Podcasts with full show notes.

D5 · This Hub

  1. Reach: ≥150 unique visitors in 14 days post-launch.
  2. Depth: ≥3 pages per session for non-bouncing visitors.
  3. Conversion: ≥35% click-through to at least one artifact page.
  4. Time on page: median ≥1:30 home, ≥2:30 artifact pages.

D6 · LinkedIn Campaign

  1. Impressions: ≥1,500 across the 5 posts.
  2. Engagement rate: ≥3% (likes + comments / impressions).
  3. Inbound: ≥3 DMs from Ontario practitioners.
  4. Referral: ≥10% of hub traffic attributable to the LinkedIn campaign in GA4.

Hub-level criteria (D5) are also stated on the home page. All thresholds tracked via GA4 with consent gating.

Limitations.

  • Sample size. N=15 is too small to generalize to "Ontario insurance marketing" as a population. Percentages are descriptive of who answered, not predictive of who didn't.
  • Selection bias. Each recruitment channel brings a distinct bias profile; warm introductions skew toward people who already trust the researcher.
  • Self-report. Familiarity, confidence, and format preference are self-reported, not behaviourally observed.
  • Single researcher. Open-ended coding was done by one person; no second-coder reliability check.
  • Time window. The data is a snapshot of Spring 2026. Industry framings of digital twins are moving quickly.
  • Audience scope. Findings speak to marketing professionals only — explicitly not sales, underwriting, or actuarial roles.
  • Industry layer. Published Canadian deployment numbers are sparse and methodologically uneven; Layer 1 is therefore directional, not quantitative.
