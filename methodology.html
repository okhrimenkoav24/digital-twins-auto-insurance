Methodology

How the project was built, who answered, what changed mid-project, what the data can and cannot tell you. Written in the order a peer reviewer would read it.

Research question

What this project asks

How can a multichannel digital media campaign be used to demonstrate the practical application and perceived value of digital twin technology to insurance marketing professionals in Ontario, Canada, based on industry data from 2022 to 2025? — Locked 2026-04-17, refined from professor-approved version

The question was narrowed in three iterations. The original framing spoke to "insurance professionals," then "marketing, sales, and underwriting," and finally "marketing professionals only." The final scope was approved by Dr. Wael Brahim on 2026-04-15.

Layer 2 — primary survey

Sample (N = 15)

The survey targeted insurance marketing professionals in Ontario, Canada. Recruitment ran from 2026-03-25 to 2026-04-22 across four channels:

  • Warm introductions (3 contacts, 6 responses).
  • LinkedIn DMs to first-degree connections in the industry (~20 sent, 4 responses).
  • Four Facebook groups: Insurance Talk Ontario Canada, Marketing for Insurance Agents, Leads Marketing and Automations, Insurance Ontario Canada (40+ direct invites, 5 responses).
  • Open call shared on the project's LinkedIn page (~0 attributable responses).

Each channel introduces a different selection bias — warm introductions skew toward people who already trust the researcher; group recruitment skews toward more publicly active practitioners. The dashboard reports results without channel-stratification because at N = 15 the strata are too small to be informative.

Closing the Assignment 1 gap

Success criteria — by artifact

Assignment 1 feedback identified that the proposal stated effectiveness in conceptual terms, with no measurable thresholds. The criteria below are the lines below which I would accept that the artifact is not working as intended. They are directional, not statistically powered.

D1 · Survey & dashboard

  1. Capability shift: a measurable reduction in the share of respondents in the "cannot explain" tier between baseline and any follow-up wave.
  2. Engagement: ≥40% of dashboard visitors scroll to the methodology section.
  3. Trust: at least 1 substantive citation or share by a peer in the period 2026-05 to 2026-06.

D2 · Digital zine

  1. Read-through: ≥60% reach 80% of zine length.
  2. Time on page: median ≥2 minutes.
  3. PDF downloads: ≥10 unique in 14 days.
  4. Comprehension: ≥70% Yes on closing question.

D3 · Simulator

  1. Completion: ≥50% complete one full scenario.
  2. Time on artifact: median ≥2 minutes.
  3. Comprehension lift: ≥5 of 15 follow-ups can describe the mechanic.

D4 · Podcast

  1. Listen-through: ≥40% completion rate.
  2. Reach: ≥50 unique plays in 14 days.
  3. Engagement: ≥3 substantive comments/DMs.

D5 · This hub

  1. Reach: ≥150 unique visitors in 14 days.
  2. Depth: ≥3 pages per session.
  3. Conversion: ≥35% click-through to an artifact.
  4. Time: median ≥1:30 home, ≥2:30 artifacts.

D6 · LinkedIn campaign

  1. Impressions: ≥1,500 across the 5 posts.
  2. Engagement rate: ≥3% (likes + comments / impressions).
  3. Inbound: ≥3 DMs from Ontario practitioners.
Process honesty

Supervisor iterations

This project shifted shape three times in response to supervisor feedback. Recording the trace because the final scope is partly a product of the iterations.

  1. 2026-04-08 — eight artifacts to six. The original plan included an infographic series and a short-form video. The infographic content was absorbed into the zine; the short-form video became the LinkedIn campaign opening posts.
  2. 2026-04-13 — narrowing the audience. Original RQ addressed "marketing, sales, and underwriting." Feedback flagged this as too broad to investigate within a 10-week capstone. The audience was narrowed to marketing professionals only.
  3. 2026-04-17 — refining the RQ. The professor-approved version contained an ambiguity that allowed the question to be answered without specifying the medium. The current version explicitly anchors the answer in a multichannel media campaign.
  4. 2026-04-30 — measurable success criteria. Assignment 1 feedback flagged that effectiveness was conceptual, not measurable. Each artifact now has explicit thresholds (above).
AI use disclosure

Where AI was used

AI tools were approved by the supervisor on 2026-04-20 for narrow purposes only: brainstorming literature themes, drafting prose for researcher review and revision, and assistance with HTML/CSS for the dashboard and this hub. All AI-generated content was reviewed, edited, and verified by the researcher before publication. Survey questions, interview prompts, recruitment messages, and methodological decisions were authored by the researcher.

A separate AI scope-extension request was submitted for Assignment 2 artifacts (zine, simulator, podcast, this hub, LinkedIn campaign) and is pending response.
