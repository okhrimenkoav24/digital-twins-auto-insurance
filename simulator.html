What it is

A research prototype, not a product.

Meet "Maria," a 34-year-old Ontario driver. Her digital twin ingests three things: telematics from a connected app, two years of claims history, and her stated preferences (deductible, optional coverage, payment cadence). The simulator lets you change one input and watch the offer change.

  • Pick an input — driving style, claim history, or a preference shift.
  • Run the twin — a parameterized model, not magic, runs behind the curtain.
  • See two offers — old (segment-based) vs new (twin-personalized).
  • Read the why — plain-language explanation of what changed and why.
Scenario · state machine

How the branching is wired.

  1. S0 — Profile load. Driver: Maria, 34, Ontario. Two years of claims history, telematics on.
  2. S1 — Pick an input. The visitor chooses one of three dimensions: driving style, claims history, or a preference shift.
  3. S2 — Twin run. The model ingests five signals: hard-braking events, night-driving share, average speed vs limit, phone use, and trip frequency.
  4. Branching. Path A returns a legacy segment-based offer. Path B returns the twin-personalized offer.
  5. S3 — Explain. The simulator renders the change in premium, the change in coverage, and a plain-English reason for both.
  6. S4 — Exit. Total runtime is about three minutes. Replay returns the visitor to S1.
Launch

Run the simulator.

Best on desktop. Mobile works but is cropped. The simulator runs entirely in your browser — nothing is sent to a server. First load pulls roughly 15–30 MB of WebGL assets.

Limitations

What this is not.

  • Research prototype, not a product. No real customer data is used.
  • Single scenario. One persona (Maria), one input dimension at a time.
  • Parameterized model. Weights are illustrative, not insurer-grade.
  • WebGL-only. A plain-text walkthrough is offered as accessibility fallback.
  • No back-end. The simulator runs entirely in the visitor's browser.

What "working" looks like

D3 success criteria.

The simulator works if…

  1. Completion: ≥50% of visitors who launch the build complete at least one full scenario run.
  2. Time on artifact: median ≥2 minutes from load to exit.
  3. Comprehension lift (qualitative): at least 5 of 15 follow-up survey contacts can describe the personalization mechanic in their own words.
  4. Accessibility: the plain-text fallback is rated "clear" by at least 80% of fallback users.
