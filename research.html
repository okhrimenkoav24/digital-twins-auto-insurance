Deliverable 1 · Research

Survey & data dashboard

Two layers of evidence on one page. Layer 1 maps Ontario auto insurance industry trends 2022–2025 from secondary sources. Layer 2 reports a primary survey of marketing practitioners (N = 15) on familiarity, barriers, format preferences, and privacy concerns around digital twin technology.

Layer 2 · Primary survey

Six findings worth your time

73%

have never heard of digital twins, or cannot explain the concept (Q4).

67%

are not, or only slightly, confident they could explain a digital twin to a colleague (Q7).

60%

name cost of implementation as the top adoption barrier (Q6).

53%

flag unclear ROI as a barrier (Q6).

80%

prefer an interactive demo or simulator format over written explainers (Q8).

73%

say privacy is "very important" or "critical" to engagement (Q9).

All percentages on N = 15. Raw responses are publicly viewable as a read-only Google Sheet — link is in the live dashboard methodology card.

What 15 responses can and cannot tell you

N = 15 is too small to generalize to "Ontario insurance marketing" as a population. The percentages on this page are descriptive of who answered, not predictive of who didn't. Recruitment was multi-channel (warm introductions, LinkedIn DMs, four Facebook groups), so each channel likely brings a different selection bias. The cost-of-implementation finding (60%) is the result that appears most stable at this sample size; the others may shift with a larger sample.

See the methodology page for sample composition, recruitment detail, and the supervisor-iteration trace that explains why the audience scope was narrowed mid-project.
